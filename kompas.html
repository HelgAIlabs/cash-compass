Market Benchmarking

Compare yourself to similar businesses to sanity-check your numbers and estimate realistic revenue. Below: who counts as a good comparison, how to reach them, and a tool to turn what you learn into a revenue estimate.

Who qualifies as a good benchmark?
Same or similar product line. A business selling the same kind of thing you do, or a close substitute. Their cost structure and pricing will be closest to yours.
Similar size and stage. Compare to businesses roughly your scale. A giant chain is not a fair mirror for a new small business.
If you sell offline: businesses in your area, or in an area with a similar income level and foot traffic. Location drives their pricing and volume.
If you sell online: businesses selling the same or similar products online, regardless of location. You compete on the same digital shelf.
How to reach them, methods that work

Direct message online

Message similar sellers on Instagram, TikTok Shop, Tokopedia, or WhatsApp Business. Keep it friendly, short, and honest that you are starting out. Most owners are happy to share a little.

Hi! I am starting a small business selling [your product] and I really admire what you have built. Would you mind sharing roughly how many units you sell in a good month, and how you landed on your price? Anything helps, thank you!

Wawancara / interview offline

Visit nearby shops or stalls in person. Buy something first, then ask a few genuine questions. Face-to-face gets richer answers than a message.

Ask about: busiest days and times, roughly how many customers per day, price range of their bestseller, and where they buy their supplies. Keep it to 5 minutes and be respectful of their time.

Observation offline

Sit near a similar shop for an hour at peak time. Count customers and note what they buy. No conversation needed, just honest counting to estimate their traffic.

Public listings online

Marketplace pages show "sold" counts, ratings, and reviews. A product with 500 reviews has sold far more than 500 units, a rough floor for their volume.

Treat every number you collect as a rough estimate, not a fact. Gather 3 to 5 comparisons and use the middle, not the best case. Never share confidential info you were not offered.
